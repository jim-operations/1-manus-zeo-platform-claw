## 1. Introduction

This document serves as a guide to the interactive network visualization of the administrative structure of a Zonal Education Office (ZEO) in Sri Lanka. This visualization, or "system blueprint," is designed to provide a clear and comprehensive map of the roles, departments, duties, and their intricate interconnections. It is intended to be a foundational tool for the development of a platform aimed at connecting relevant parties within the educational administration system.

The interactive graph is built upon extensive research into the functions of ZEOs, drawing from official government documents, research papers, and reports from international organizations. It provides a dynamic and explorable model of the flow of responsibility and communication within a typical ZEO. This blueprint has been specifically updated to reflect the administrative structure of ZEO Embilipitiya, where the position is designated as **Deputy Director (Development)**.

## 2. Understanding the Visualization

The visualization is an interactive, force-directed graph. You can interact with it in the following ways:

*   **Click and Drag:** Move nodes around to get a better view of their connections.
*   **Zoom and Pan:** Use your mouse wheel to zoom in and out, and click and drag the background to pan across the network.
*   **Click on a Node:** Clicking on any node will open a sidebar on the right, displaying detailed information about that entity and a list of its direct connections.
*   **Search:** Use the search bar at the top right to find specific nodes by name or description.
*   **Filter by Category:** Click on the items in the legend at the bottom left to highlight all nodes belonging to a specific category.

## 3. The Data Model: Node Categories

The network consists of nodes representing different entities within the ZEO ecosystem. Each node is color-coded according to its category. The following table details each category and its significance:

| Category | Color | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Leadership Positions** | Orange | These are the highest-ranking officials within the ZEO, such as the Zonal Director and Deputy Directors. They hold the primary decision-making and oversight responsibilities. |
| **Departments / Divisions** | Blue | These are the core administrative units within the ZEO, each responsible for a broad functional area (e.g., Finance, Education Development). |
| **Staff Roles** | Green | These represent specific job roles within the departments, such as In-Service Advisors (ISAs) and Administrative Officers. |
| **Duties / Functions** | Purple | These nodes represent the specific tasks and responsibilities performed by the various departments and roles (e.g., Teacher Transfers, Budget Management). |
| **External Entities** | Red | These are government bodies and organizations that interact with the ZEO but are outside of its direct command structure (e.g., Ministry of Education, Provincial Department of Education). |
| **Programs / Initiatives** | Pink | These represent major national or provincial programs that the ZEO is responsible for implementing (e.g., Programme for School Improvement, General Education Modernization Project). |
| **Stakeholders** | Cyan | These are the individuals and groups who are directly affected by the ZEO's work, such as teachers, students, and parents. |

## 4. Understanding the Connections

The lines (or "edges") connecting the nodes represent relationships and flows of responsibility. The labels on these lines describe the nature of the interaction. For example:

*   A line from the **Zonal Director** to a **Deputy Director** labeled "oversees" indicates a direct reporting relationship.
*   A line from the **Establishment & General Management** department to the **Teacher Transfers** duty labeled "performs" shows that this department is responsible for carrying out that function.
*   A line from the **Provincial Department of Education** to the **Zonal Director** labeled "directs & funds" illustrates the flow of authority and financial resources.

By exploring these connections, you can trace the path of any given administrative process, identify the key personnel involved, and understand where responsibility ultimately lies.

## 5. How to Use This Blueprint for Your Platform

This interactive blueprint can be a powerful tool for designing your platform. Here are some ways you can leverage it:

*   **User Role Definition:** The different node categories (Leadership, Staff Roles, Stakeholders) can directly inform the user roles and permission levels within your platform.
*   **Workflow Mapping:** Trace the connections related to a specific duty (e.g., "Student Admissions") to map out the entire workflow, from the initial request to the final approval. This can help you design digital forms and automated processes.
*   **Identifying Key Contacts:** For any given issue or inquiry, the graph can help identify the responsible department or individual. Your platform can use this logic to direct user queries to the correct person.
*   **Building a Knowledge Base:** The descriptions associated with each node can be used to build a comprehensive, searchable knowledge base within your platform, explaining the functions of the ZEO to users.

This visualization provides the foundational understanding of the ZEO's complex administrative web, enabling you to build a platform that is not only technologically sound but also accurately reflects the real-world processes and relationships within Sri Lanka's education system.
